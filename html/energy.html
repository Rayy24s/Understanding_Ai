Energy Crisis

Global Water Bankruptcy

Water is a foundational pillar of human development and planetary stability.

Yet 2.2 billion people lack safely managed drinking water. At the same time, water-hungry extractive industries, water demanding electricity generation plants and increasingly thirsty data centres have put even more pressure on strained resources. Data centres accounted for around 1% to 2% of global electricity demand in 2020.

All of the power being generated must be cooled and while some companies have experimented with using cool air on mountain sites others turn to water-based cooling methods further increasing water consumption.

In 2014, the amount of electricity being used to cool systems roughly equal to the amount necessary to power the system approximately 194 terawatt-hours which is comparable to the annual energy consumption of Argentina. This highlights a wider issue that technological expansion problem often prioritizes growth and profit over environmental sustainability.

AI's Energy Consumption

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is unable to exist without energy.

Specifically, large amounts of electricity and these demands are met by data centres which use huge amounts of electricity. Impact regions which house data centres such as Dallas Texas experience disproportionately high energy demand. In Ireland, data centres consume around 20% of metered electricity supply and in the Virginia, USA, that figure grows to 25% this not only puts strain on resources but increases cost and lowers supply for people living in those regions.
